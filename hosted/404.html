While oversleeping can be a concern, so too is undersleeping.

When you wake up, there's a brief period of keeping your eyes closed, just embracing the comfort of your bed- a comfort that'll never be achieved when you're trying to fall asleep, and seems only to exist in the need to sleep.

These decisions spread out, upon waking. Do you sleep in, do you brush your teeth? Perhaps you'll make coffee or eat breakfast? Read email or even go for a jog. Perhaps you'll take a shower or even all those things.

I believe that the first thing we do in the mornings defines our priorities in life- cleanliness, knowledge, physical fitness, order, self-gratification. However this, too is debatable- instead, might we be defined by what we do after our awaking ritual is complete. Are you a student, who slaves away in endless education? Are you an employee, who slaves away in the endless monotony of your job? Or perhaps you're free from work, from school, from societal expectation and money, deemed the least valuable to society but perhaps the most free?

Can't you just go back to sleep?
