While oversleeping can be a concern, so too is undersleeping.

When you wake up, there's a brief period of keeping your eyes closed, just embracing the comfort of your bed- a comfort that'll never be achieved when you're trying to fall asleep, and seems only to exist in the need to sleep.

These decisions spread out, upon waking. Do you sleep in, do you brush your teeth? Perhaps you'll make coffee or eat breakfast? Read email or even go for a jog. Perhaps you'll take a shower or even all those things.

You believe that the first thing you do in the morning defines your priorities in life- cleanliness, knowledge, physical fitness, order, self-gratification. However this, too is debatable- instead, might you be defined by what you do after our awakening ritual is complete. Are you a student, who slaves away in endless education? Are you an employee, who slaves away in the endless monotony of your job? Or perhaps you're free from work, from school, from societal expectation and money, deemed the least valuable to society but perhaps the most free?

Can't you just go back to sleep?
